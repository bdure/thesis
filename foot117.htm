Newspaper Association of America, "Newspaper Readership Remains Competitive in Top 50 Markets," available from http://www.naa.org/about/news/10_cmifall99.html; Internet — the study found that the percentage of people in the top 50 American media markets watching prime-time broadcast television has fallen from 45.3 percent in spring 1997 to 38.5 percent in fall 1999. The percentage of the same sample that reads a daily newspaper has fallen from 58.8 percent to 56.9 percent over the same period.
