The Economist, "Truth, Lies and Cyberspace," The Economist, April 24, 1999, p. 84. The article listed three more notable hoaxes. First, someone outside of Bloomberg's news organization posted a Web page designed to resemble a page in the Bloomberg Web site to that site. Second, an e-mail passed around the Internet wrongly claimed that fashion designer Tommy Hilfiger had made a racist statement in an interview with talk-show host Oprah Winfrey. Finally, another e-mail wrongly claimed that pop singer Mariah Carey, commenting in USA TODAY on the death of Jordan's King Hussein, had confused the king with basketball player Michael Jordan.
